Cyberbullying-I Need Help

Although cyberbullying is prevalent among all ages across the world, it is especially common among teenagers (13-19) due to several reasons. First of all, given that human’s prefrontal cortex is not fully developed until the age of mid 20s, which is the part of the brain that regulates people’s thoughts, actions, and emotions, teenagers have a harder time controlling their emotions, making them especially vulnerable to harsh languages and bullies. I have been volunteering as a listener at 7 Cups, where I listen to teenagers with different kinds of concerns and create an outlet for their emotions. Many of the people I listened to face the problem of being bullied, especially cyberbullying. In addition to the physical characteristics of teens, cyberbullying is also worsened due to governmental policies. For example, the recent Supreme Court case on June 29, 2023 rules against affirmative action which states that race is considered as a part of the admission process. Although this ban might be good in terms of a higher chance to get into a top school for Asians, it has the potential to foster cyberbullying targeted to Asian students, and in a worse situation, it can help to contribute to the anti-Asian movement and the Asian hate due to the idea that Asians are taking away their opportunities to study in top schools. Those Asian teenagers, though didn’t do anything wrong themselves, have to deal with all the potential cyberbullying and consequences of the government’s decision.
